How to Maintain a Rock-Face Foundation in Namibia

Category: Maintenance & Repairs | Published: October 2024

Rock-face foundations are popular in Namibia due to their strength and resistance to soil movement. However, proper maintenance is essential for long-term durability.

Key Maintenance Tips

  • Inspect annually: Check for cracks after rainy seasons. Small hairline cracks are often normal, but larger ones need attention.
  • Ensure proper drainage: Make sure water flows away from the foundation. Pooling water can soften the soil and compromise stability.
  • Watch the vegetation: Avoid planting large trees near foundation walls. Roots can grow underneath and cause uplift or cracking.
  • Seal cracks early: If you see visible cracks, seal them early to prevent moisture penetration which can rust reinforcement steel.

Pro Tip: Homes in areas like Okahandja and Osona benefit greatly from rock-face foundations because of the specific soil stability conditions and the cost-efficiency of this method compared to deep raft foundations.
