How Long Does It Take to Build a House in Namibia?

Category: Construction 101

Patience is key when building a home. While every project is unique, here is a realistic timeline for a standard family home in Namibia.

Typical Timeline Breakdown

  • Planning & Approvals (1–3 Months): This includes finalizing drawings, engineering sign-offs, and municipal approval.
  • Construction (4–6 Months): From site clearing and foundations to roofing and finishes.
  • Final Inspections (2–4 Weeks): Snag lists, final municipal inspections, and handover.

Factors That Affect Speed

Weather (especially the rainy season), material availability, and design complexity can all impact the schedule. Plot-and-plan homes are generally faster to build than custom designs.
