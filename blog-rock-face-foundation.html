How to Maintain a Rock-Face Foundation in Namibia

Category: Maintenance & Repairs

Why Rock-Face Foundations Are Popular

Rock-face foundations are widely used in Namibia due to soil conditions and durability. They offer excellent stability in areas like Okahandja and Osona Village, where soil movement can be a concern.

Maintenance Tips

  • Inspect cracks annually: Check the foundation walls after the rainy season. Hairline cracks are often normal, but widening cracks need professional attention.
  • Ensure proper drainage: Water pooling around your foundation is the biggest enemy. Ensure the ground slopes away from your home.
  • Seal minor cracks early: Use a high-quality sealant to prevent moisture from penetrating the foundation and rusting the reinforcement steel.
  • Manage vegetation: Keep large trees and shrubs away from the foundation wall to prevent root damage.

Final Advice

Professional inspections every few years ensure structural longevity. If you notice significant movement or cracking, consult a structural engineer immediately.
