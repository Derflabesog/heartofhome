Building a home is a major investment. Here are practical ways to keep costs down without cutting corners on structural integrity.

1. Keep the Design Simple

Complex rooflines and irregular shapes increase labor and material costs. A simple rectangular or square footprint is the most economical to build and roof.

2. Choose Standard Sizes

Design your rooms to fit standard material sizes (like tiles, carpets, and ceiling boards) to minimize waste. Custom-sized windows and doors are significantly more expensive than standard aluminum sizes available in Namibia.

3. Buy Local

Imported finishes look great but come with high transport costs. Namibia produces excellent quality bricks and cement. Using locally sourced materials supports the economy and saves you money.
