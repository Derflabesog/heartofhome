Before you lay a single brick, you need permission. Navigating the municipal approval process in Namibia can be daunting, but it's essential for a legal build.

The Process

  1. Drafting: A registered architect or draftsperson must draw your plans according to SANS 10400 building standards.
  2. Submission: Plans are submitted to your local municipality (e.g., City of Windhoek or Okahandja Municipality) along with scrutiny fees.
  3. Review: Departments such as Town Planning, Water & Waste, and Fire Safety review the plans.
  4. Approval: Once all departments sign off, you receive an approved set of plans. Only then can construction begin.

Common Delays

Plans are often rejected due to building line encroachments or insufficient ventilation. Working with an experienced team ensures your plans are compliant from the start.
