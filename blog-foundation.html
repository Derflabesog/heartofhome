Namibia's soil conditions vary greatly, from sandy terrain in coastal areas to rocky ground in the central highlands. Choosing the right foundation is critical for the longevity of your home.

Why Soil Testing Matters

Before pouring concrete, a geotechnical investigation is essential. This reveals the soil's bearing capacity and potential for expansion, which can crack standard foundations.

Rock-Face Foundations

In areas like Windhoek and Okahandja, rock-face foundations are popular. They provide excellent stability on uneven, rocky terrain and add a distinct aesthetic appeal to the property.

Raft vs. Strip Foundations

For sandy or clay-heavy soils, a raft foundation (a single concrete slab) spreads the load evenly, reducing the risk of sinking. Strip foundations are more common for stable ground but require careful excavation.
